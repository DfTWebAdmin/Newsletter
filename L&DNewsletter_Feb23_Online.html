Welcome to the DfT Comms L&D Newsletter!


Landing in your inboxes every month, this will be your one stop shop for all things learning and development. From Government Communication Service (GCS) and Civil Service Learning courses to other training opportunities, we hope this will be a useful one stop shop for all your L&D needs.


Remember to sign up to GCS Connect if you haven’t already done so, where you can access all GCS courses. Further details can be found below.


Remember there is budget for L&D, so if there’s any training you’re interested in that requires funding please speak to your line manager and SLT lead who can look into this for you.

A good starting point is having a look at the ‘Essential learning for line managers’ page on MyDfT which sets out some of the recommended and mandatory courses for managers.

We’re pairing people up from different grades and teams across the directorate each month, to chat informally about leadership related topics and ask any burning questions. You can learn from someone more senior and, equally, get a chance to reverse mentor senior colleagues and share your thoughts and experience with them.

Our topic for this month’s coffee roulette is ‘Having open conversations in teams’, to coincide with ‘Time to Talk Day’s' that takes place in February.

The first pairings have been sent out, please check your emails! If you'd like to be involved and haven't received an email, please get in touch and we'll get you added!

You can find out more about GCS here. It includes the GCS curriculum and standards of professional practice, guidance and case studies, information about training and resources, mentoring opportunities and accelerated development programmes.

To sign up to access courses and training run by GCS, use GCS Connect. All you need to do is create an account!

A range of courses can also be accessed through Civil Service Learning and are a great opportunity to further develop your skills. Not all courses are free so please speak to your line manager before registering for any which have fees.

You can also access your mandatory training courses through Civil Service Learning, and it will prompt you to undertake these as and when required.
